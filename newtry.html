02. Campaign Strategy
Themes and Tone
Armed with the insights from our comprehensive outreach and research, we initiated our fall semester campaign with a clear and purposeful first step: establishing our tone and themes. This foundation was crucial to ensure that every message, event, and interaction resonated with our core mission of inclusivity and empowerment.
  • We decided to adopt a theme titled "Cloud 9." This theme was chosen to symbolize the aspirational journey of leadership that ΔΟΖ belives in
  • This theme aligns with the experiences of college students who are navigating their own paths towards personal and professional growth.
  • Furthermore, it is a lighthearted and attractive approach that engages students without intimidation

Website
In designing our website, we took to heart the findings from our research, aiming to directly address and incorporate the community's feedback into every aspect of the site. Our primary focus was on creating an environment that mirrored our dedication to inclusivity and empowerment, ensuring that the website itself became a central place for these values. We focused on:
  • User-Friendly Design: Recognizing the intimidation factor, navigation was simplified, making it easy for visitors to find information to lower the barrier to entry.
  • Diverse Representation: We ensured that the imagery and stories featured on the website showcased the diversity of our community. From the visuals to the testimonials, every element was chosen to reflect the wide range of backgrounds.
  • Engagement Opportunities: We integrated features that allow for direct participation and feedback from users.
  • Events Calendar: To ensure that our website was a central hub for all our activities, we added a comprehensive events calendar.

Outreach Events
Planning the recruitment events involved strategic thinking and creative approaches to engage potential members. Here's an overview of some events, along with the rationale applied to ensure their success:

  • PowerPoint Night "Getting to Know the Chapter": A fun and informal evening where current and potential members presented short PowerPoint slides about their experiences. We utilized Storytelling to create connection with attendees. This approach aimed to humanize the organization, making it more relatable.

  • Tacos and Trivia: An interactive event with free tacos and trivia about leadership and pop culture. We employed Engagement Marketing by creating an enjoyable atmosphere, fostering community spirit.

  • Tabling Outside with Donuts: We Set up a welcoming table outside high-traffic areas on campus, offering free donuts and information. I applied the Principle of Reciprocity from Influence Marketing,to offer something of value for free (donuts) in encouraging people to learn about our organization and potentially join.
